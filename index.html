About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in Electrical Engineering at the University of Massachusetts Lowell, working under the guidance of Dr. Hengyong Yu, FIEEE. My research focuses on probabilistic methods in machine learning and developing deep generative models for inverse problems, particularly in medical imaging.


My current work centers on designing generative models that capture the uncertainty and structures of real-world data while supporting efficient and reliable probabilistic reasoning. I'm particularly interested in developing efficient diffusion transformers for real clinical few-view CT reconstruction and integrating Large Language Models for medical AI image applications.


With experience across multiple research institutions and a strong foundation in deep learning frameworks and methodologies, I am passionate about pushing the boundaries of what's possible with generative AI in healthcare contexts.

Research Experience

Diffusion Models for CT Reconstruction

Imaging and Informatics Lab, University of Massachusetts Lowell | 2023-Present

Developing state-of-the-art diffusion models to reconstruct high-quality CT images from limited-angle scans. This work addresses a critical challenge in medical imaging where radiation dose reduction is paramount, but image quality cannot be compromised.


Currently integrating large language models with diffusion models to guide vision tasks using text prompts, exploring the potential of multimodal learning in medical imaging.


Authoring a journal paper on combining diffusion and transformer techniques for CT image generation, with promising preliminary results showing significant improvements over traditional methods.

AI for Cardiac MRI Analysis

Shao's Lab, University of Florida | 2023

Developed and validated an AI model for automatic segmentation of the four cardiac chambers on CMR images, improving diagnostic efficiency and accuracy.


Built a predictive AI model to identify CMR radiomic signatures of cardiac sarcoidosis, allowing for earlier detection of this challenging condition.

Multi-view Tracking with Transformers

Computer Vision and Sensing Systems Lab, University of Florida | 2022-2023

Employed an unsupervised learning framework (SimCLR) to train the DETR backbone for enhanced inter-view connectivity in multi-camera scenarios.


Developed a transformer model capable of tracking individuals across different camera views for airport safety applications, addressing the challenging problem of re-identification across viewpoints.

GAN-based CT Denoising

Xidian University | 2020-2021

Proposed an unpaired training data method using coupled-GANs for low-dose CT denoising with dual generative networks and discriminators.


Prepared a research paper for submission on the proposed denoising method, demonstrating significant image quality improvements while maintaining structural fidelity.
